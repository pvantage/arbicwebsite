Biostatistics Course

take this course

Description: Biostatistics plays an essential role in all stages of a quantitative health care research project from design through to analysis and interpretation. This 2 days intensive course covers the essential principles and methods required. Emphasis is on study design, appropriate analysis, and interpretation of results. The underlying concepts of statistical analysis as well as basic analysis techniques are covered. Sessions include lectures and practical work, both computer based and using small workshops for discussion.

Course objectives: The course aims to teach health care researchers the essential elements of biostatistics. The focus is mainly on interpretation and understanding appropriate analyses. The course is also aim to teach SPSS statistical software. At the end of the course participants will have acquired the essential skills for performing and understanding basic statistical analysis using SPSS . In particular participants will have gained an understanding of:
1. Understand the role of statistical methods in biomedical science.
2. The types of data generated in research studies.
3. The most common methods of analysis for categorical and continuous data.
4. Summarize data appropriately, select and apply appropriate basic methods of statistical inference.
5. Interpret the results of analyses in a clear, concise and logical way.

Entry Requirements: The course is relevant to all health care professionals or anyone in the health care field who needs to understand, use or carry out health care research studies. Material covered is of an introductory level and provides some references and foundation for more advanced techniques. The course assumes a passing familiarity with the statistical terms used in the medical literature but does not assume any deep understanding.
